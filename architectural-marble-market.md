<p><p>The architectural marble market consists of two primary types: natural marble and artificial marble. Natural marble is a metamorphic rock formed from limestone, renowned for its unique patterns and colors, making it popular in high-end architectural applications. In contrast, artificial marble, often made from crushed stone and resin, offers more uniformity in appearance and cost-effectiveness, appealing to budget-conscious projects. Both types serve distinct purposes in construction, catering to varying aesthetic preferences and functional requirements within the architectural landscape.</p></p>
