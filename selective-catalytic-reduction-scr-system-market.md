<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) System is an advanced technology used to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions from internal combustion engines. This system typically involves injecting a urea solution into the exhaust stream, which reacts with NOx in the presence of a catalyst, converting it into harmless nitrogen and water. With increasing regulatory pressures for cleaner emissions, SCR systems have gained prominence in various industries, particularly automotive and power generation.</p><p>The SCR System Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period. <p><p>The Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) system market comprises three main types: NH3-SCR, Urea-SCR, and Ammonia-SCR. NH3-SCR utilizes ammonia directly as a reducing agent to convert nitrogen oxides into harmless nitrogen and water. Urea-SCR employs urea as a non-toxic ammonia source, which hydrolyzes to release ammonia and effectively reduce emissions. Ammonia-SCR refers to systems primarily using ammonia solutions for the same reduction process. These types cater to various industrial applications and regulatory emission standards, driving market demand.</p></p>
