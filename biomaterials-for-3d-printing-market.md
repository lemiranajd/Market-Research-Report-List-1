<p><p>The biomaterials for 3D printing market comprises three primary types: ceramics, polymers, and composites. Ceramics are known for their biocompatibility and mechanical strength, making them suitable for bone implants and dental applications. Polymers offer flexibility and ease of processing, ideal for soft tissue scaffolds and medical devices. Composites combine the properties of both ceramics and polymers, enhancing mechanical performance and functionality for various biomedical applications. Together, these materials drive innovation in tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.</p></p>
